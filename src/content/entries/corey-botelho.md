## Overview

Corey James Botelho, a former Armstrong County, Pennsylvania sheriff's deputy, soccer coach, and firearms/tactical business owner who publicly promoted anti-government and anti-vaccine views, was convicted in April 2025 of multiple felony child-rape charges and sentenced to 30–60 years in state prison. The court declared him a sexually violent predator subject to lifetime sex-offender registration.

## Description

A Pennsylvania State Police investigation launched in December 2023 documented a pattern of sexual assaults against a minor relative that spanned approximately seven years, occurring between when the victim was 8 and 15 years old. A jury convicted Botelho on all counts after a day-and-a-half trial, including rape of a child, multiple counts of rape by forcible compulsion, involuntary deviate sexual intercourse with a child, and aggravated indecent assault of a child — all first-degree felonies.
