## Overview

Conservative radio host and OutKick founder Clay Travis has repeatedly downplayed sexual abuse of minors on-air. In July 2025, he defended Jeffrey Epstein's trafficking of teenage girls as 'actually legal' in some states because Epstein's victims were 'not 9 and 10-year-old girls.' His 2008 book Man: The Book contained the line 'Age of consent is only an issue if you actually have sex. Looking is no crime.'

## Description

On his program OutKick the Show in July 2025, Travis argued that Epstein's serial sexual abuse of minors — which included victims as young as 14 — would have been legal in states like Alabama where the age of consent is 16. He concluded that 'Jeffrey Epstein was engaged with 16, 17, 18-year-old girls to the extent that they were underage, not 9 and 10-year-old girls.' Epstein's trafficking victims were well-documented as minors younger than the age of consent in any U.S. state. Media Matters has separately documented Travis telling listeners that his 'credo' is 'all women are sluts' and a long pattern of misogynistic commentary. This entry documents public commentary defending abusers, not criminal conduct by Travis himself.
