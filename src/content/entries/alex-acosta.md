## Overview

Alex Acosta is a Republican former U.S. attorney and later Trump's labor secretary whose handling of the Jeffrey Epstein case produced the notorious non-prosecution agreement that shielded Epstein from federal prosecution and protected potential co-conspirators.

## Description

Released Epstein archive documents repeatedly identify R. Alexander Acosta as the U.S. attorney who approved and signed off on Jeffrey Epstein's 2007 non-prosecution agreement in South Florida. The archive includes the agreement itself, letters laying out plea terms, and later Justice Department review material examining how Acosta's office handled the case. The released OPR material says investigators did not find proof of improper favoritism, but it explicitly criticizes Acosta's judgment in resolving the federal investigation through the non-prosecution agreement. This entry documents Acosta as an abuse-enablement and plea-deal figure in the Epstein case, not as a direct perpetrator of the underlying sex crimes.
